Pilot Project

What's the ADDIE Model?

There are plenty of methods to make a project; whether you start a project by yourself or with a team, you must prepare by having an action plan to follow. Design principles and learning models will be your friend in the process. A well-known learning model is ADDIE; read about it below. Consider your future project and see how ADDIE fits when developing a road map for your project.



Step 1: Analysis

The first step of the ADDIE model is to analyse. You can start your project by having a meeting with the SME and discuss important factors that will turn into your needs analysis.


Step 2: Design

The second step of the ADDIE model is to design. During this step, you can proceed to draft and plan the design of the project for the SME. Taking in consideartion the notes and feedback obtained from the needs analyis.

Step 3: Develop

The third step of the ADDIE model is to develop. In this step, you can take your design and turn it into reality! Depending on the project, you can develop the project in the software of your choice.

Step 4: Implement

On the fourth step, you can implement the developed project you've design to the learners. It will help gather feedback for the last step.

Step 5: Evaluate

The final step is to evaluate the project. The evaluation will help you give correct data to the SME about the impact of the project by doing these two things with your results:

  • Formative Evaluation
  • Summative Evaluation
